Savic Motorcycles

Savic Motorcycles was born in Melbourne in 2016 with the creation of a crude prototype. Fast-forward to 2025 and this company has now secured funding enough to start manufacture of their electric motorcyles in a facility based in West Melbourne. They offer products that are fully customisable, so each bike has a touch of uniqueness to it (Savic Motorcycles, 2025). Our department commenced our review process with Savic in 2024 and will continue to follow their progress as they aim to bring innovative disruption to the industry. We seek to promote all forms of E-transport here at the department, especially those from local companies that can provide local jobs.


What products have we reviewed?

As part of our review process, we consider the range of E-transport options a company has available and also review these separately. The aim of this is to ensure each product we promote is safe for use and is either low-emission or emission free. You can view Savic's reviewed products below:

  • Alpha

    • The Alpha model is part of Savic's C-Series and is the luxury form of their current offering. The series has an impressive range of features, including multiple in-built ride modes, built-in cellular connectivity and can be charged anywhere you can charge a mobile phone. All C-Series bikes also come with regenerative braking, which means that the energy generated from the braking process is fed back into the battery. The Alpha has a range of 200km on one charge and can reach speeds of 0-100km/hour in 3.5 seconds (Savic Motorcycles, 2025).

  • Delta

    • The Delta model is the more economy version of Savic's C-Series but it offers the same impressive range of features as the Alpha. In addition to these features it also includes an extendable pillion seat, a liquid cooling system, touchpad screen and no gears or clutch. With a range of 150km and reaching speeds of 0-100km in 4.5 seconds, the Delta model offers comparable specs to the Alpha at a fraction of the price (Savic Motorcycles, 2025).
